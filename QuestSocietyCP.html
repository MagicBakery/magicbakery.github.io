1.2.1 Gaia Share

Ivy:

Gaia Share is an ethical accounting of resources to allow fundamental autonomy.

 Ivy:

If you want a city that only allows cat lovers, you are free to start one with your Gaia shares, and let cat-lovers join with their Gaia shares for a continuous cat-lovers zone.

What you can't do, is to prevent cat lovers from leaving and withdrawing their shares as they leave.

 Ivy:

Quest Society does not fail this principle. You can withdraw. It is normal. It is a choice.

[ğŸ² Why not random?]â†´
ğŸ”
Ivy:

One might ask, if two people are trying to get the same house, why not just toss a coin and decide it randomly?

 Ivy:

The people in conflict may indeed decide to settle it by a coin toss, then neither would need to spend only honor.

 Ivy:

The question is about why does Quest Society allow one party to veto the coin toss and compete with honor instead.

 Ivy:

The reason is that deciding it randomly wastes an opportunity to incentivize people to help others.

 Ivy:

In a Quest Society, if you want to compete, you must first have helped and earned credits.

 Ivy:

If it is just random, people who choose to help no one and spend all their time waiting in line for each opportunity to roll the dice would end up getting most of the good houses.

 Ivy:

This explanation can also be the response to why it is not "first come first serve": because first come first serve heavily reward people who only compete without helping anyone.

 Ivy:

Quest Society is designed to reward those who are busy helping others. Their contribution should not be defeated by bad luck or by those who only complete.

Rewarding Care

1.4 Rewarding Care

1.4 Rewarding Care

ğŸ¦‰
Ivy:

A quest society is a society that organizes its needs as quests that people can choose to complete and earn credits, and does so in a way that promotes peace without violating any ethical principle.

 Ivy:

Quest Society has a goal to help people be consciously good, but does not require that to function or demand people to be consciously good.

 Ivy:

While it is nice if people genuine understand it, Quest Society does not rely on a mass understanding of how it works.

 Ivy:

When the correct laws and infrastructures are in place, it is not bad if the vast majority of the people simply follow what the system offers.

 Ivy:

This is similar to saying while it is nice if everyone understands calculus, a modern society does not require everyone to understand it to reap the benefit of calculus.

 Ivy:

Keep education free and accessible, so anyone who wants to learn it can learn, but don't design a system that relies on people to understand advanced subjects to function or be stable.

 Ivy:

A good system should remain functional if the most learned population is suddenly wiped out, and the less learned population can still follow good habits and practices to do the right thing even when they don't fully understand the reasons, while new generation catch up in the lost knowledge.
