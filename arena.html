4. Dungeon Challenge

Assess your level as a philosopher and upgrade yourself.

ğŸ¥š 4.0. 0-Star

You are at 0-star if you dislike or is apathetic to philosophy. You just want a routine, follow the rules, or just do whatever you want without examining or seeking any principles.

To advance to the next level, is to cultivate an interest in thinking and seeking principles.

In the dungeon game, your curiousity to enter the dungeon is enough to reach 1-star.

ğŸ£ 4.1. 1-Star

You are at 1-star if you are curious about thinking.

At 1-stars, you may hold on to some concepts strongly, but are unable to define what they mean to others.

You tend to use words in self-serving ways, such as describing what you do as good, but others acting the same way as bad.

Your arguments are often circular or self-contradictory.

Your "arguments" may only involve using praiseful adjectives when you describe what you want, and using blameful adjectives to describe what others want.

To advance to the next level, you need to be able to define your words in ways that can be objectively evaluated.

Your definitions should allow others to tell what meets and what does not meet the definition.

Your definitions do not need to be universally accepted, neutral, nor "politically correct", but they should be universally applicable, such as regardless of who is speaking.

To be clear, none of the star-ranking require the word choice be universally accepted, neutral, or politically correct. That is not a matter of philosophical sophistication but communication sophistication, which is not what this dungeon is about.

To be universally applicable at the 2-Star level, your definition must rely on explicit conditions or properties that any third party can check for presence or absence, thereby removing reliance on the speaker's personal feeling or internal, unstated bias. This allows for objective debate. It does not, at this level, require the definition to be resilient or useful outside of a specific context.

Any philosopher at 3-star or higher would give courtesy of definition and not require a speaker to use neutral terms. They focus on understanding the intended meaning, not how it is said.

ğŸ¤ 4.2. 2-Star

At 2-star you are able to make definitions that meet objective distinction, but you still have trouble juggle more than one set of definitions when you talk to other philosophers.

You can discuss only with philosophers that share your same terminology, but when you talk to philosophers using different terminologies, you fail at interpreting them based on their definitions, and you end up making strawman arguments that you fail to acknowledge.

Other philosophies seem easy to defeat to you only because you misunderstood them; or you feel justified to dismiss other philosophy only because they don't share your definition.

When philosophers of higher levels point out this problem of yours, you often project their criticism back to them. But in reality they understand you, but disagree with you; while you don't realize that you don't understand them enough to decide whether you agree or not.

To advance to the next level, you must be able to understand what others mean using their definitions.

ğŸ•Šï¸ 4.3. 3-Star

At 3-star, you have attained courtesy of definition where you allow others to define words and you are able to understand other philosophies they way they are intended.

But at 3-star, you fail at considering stability and robustness of a philosophy.

You focus on a rosy path where everything is in the right condition for you philosophy to work, and dismiss critiques of the fagility of your philosophy.

Your arguments involves cherry picking the context to show "it can work", and you do not address the contexts where it doesn't work.

To advance to the next level is to cultivate an intention to seek philsophies that are resilient.

ğŸ¦‰ 4.4. 4-Star

At 4-star, you are able to address variations and disturbances to attain closed-loop stability. But you do so by assuming that they need to be done effortfully.

You focus on the necessity of personal or societal duty as requirements for the system to handle the disturbances.

Stability to you has a cost. To advance, you need to pursue philosophies that can achieve stability without a cost.

ğŸ¦… 4.5. 5-Star

At 5-star, you understand closed-loop systems and stabilities enough to reroute disturbance using anti fragile concepts so that they become ingredients instead of costs to the system.

Your philosophy has no dependency on individual or societal duty to make it work; it is baked into the philosophy that it works.

Your philosophy acts like a catalyst that makes things work, and no one needs to lose.

It is at this level that you have found the Philosopher's Stone, the Catalyse for more good things to come at no cost.

Finding the Philosopher's Stone is not the end of a philosopher's journey, but the beginning to be able to do philosopher quests to explore different methods to do philosophical alchemy.

Up until 5-star, you have only been catching up to where the world needs philsophers to be. Now you are where the world needs philosophers for to see long lasting, anti-fragile solutions.

In the dungeon at Room 5 where the Philosopher's Stone is attained, the dungeon master should invite the challenger to quest for better solutions and share them.
