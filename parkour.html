1 Challenge

In a discussion, oftentimes people get distracted and end up talking a lot without making progress on the topic.

To help bring awareness, the transcript of a discussion can be analyzed to highlight what went well and what went badly.

1.1. Gamified Curation

To make the training fun, imagine that the participants of a discussion are on an obstacle course in an ancient ruin dungeon that has traps. Would they succeed at reaching the treasures?

To curate a discussion in this gamified way, consider what the purpose of the discussion is, and define success on a scale like this:

5-star: The participant reaches the goal efficiently and helps others along the way.

4-star: The participant hasn't made good progress toward the goal, and gives pointers on where to explore to make further progress.

3-star: The participant is helping to make progress, although they have found a deadend or does not know how to get pass an obstacle.

2-star: The participant is not trying to make progress, but to recap the discussion so that others might join and try.

1-star: The participant is interested in the subject enough to join, but they might be interested in a tangential aspect that doesn't progress the main thread.

0-star: The participant doesn't act like a participant but more like a spectator, cheering or booing someone else without engaging with the topic themself.

When you curate a participant's trajectory, be mindful that some detours might still get something useful to further the main thread. The participant might find a principle while focusing on an aspect that does not seem related to the main thread at first, but is only clear that it was beneficial in retrospect.

So when you curate, you shouldn't discourage those who explore a side room on their own, but you could be fair in calling foul when a participant is delaying or obstructing others from making progress.

1.2. Goal

To identify the goal of a discussion, always refer to the highest ethics including the considerations of Quest Society Ethics in https://magicbakery.github.io/ethics.html.

This is necessary because sometimes the person who starts the discussion (the OP) does not pose the purpose at the highest ethics, then even if the discussion is "resolved" according to the OP, true progress is not made.

To rate the participants, you must do so based on the highest treasure that can be obtained from the topic.

The curator should celebrate those who notice hidden gems and going for them.

1.3. Ongoing

If the goal of the discussion is not reached yet, give a summary of the highest goal of the discussion, and encourage more people to join and help them catch up to make progress.
