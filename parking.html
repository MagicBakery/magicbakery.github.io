Parking

1. Ethical Goals

This section highlights the ethical considerations for parking policies.

🏰 1.1. Autonomy

Since an option zone is opt-in and can set any rules, for this node we assume that the context is the common zone, where the underlying rules should be universal that can resolve conflicts in autonomy.

For those who are not familiar, please refer to another node on how to turn a common zone into an option zone.

🪨 1.2. Negative Externalities

For the common zone, the main consideration should be negative externalities such as pollution.

A person should contain or resolve the effects of their travel, leaving no pollution.

🕊️ 1.3. Access

The purpose of common roads should be to help people move around so they can end up with where they want to be.

Be mindful that when people are already where they want to be, and roads shouldn't be designed so that people "have to" use them.

Also, roads as an infrastructure to help people move, shouldn't be seen as a means to force people to fund other things.

🪵 1.4. Antifragile

Antifragile roads are roads that not only require no "maintenance", but the more it is used the better it gets.

Antifragile parking is parking that gets better when it is used.

🌱 1.4.1. Gaia Accounting

At the policy level, People normally have an incentive to provide space for travelers and visitors to park because when they badge in to the space you steward, they are micro-funding your stewardship and freeing your Gaia Shares.

In a crowded town area of a common zone, it is the individual stewards that decide how much space they want their space to be visitor parkings.

A downtown business steward would therefore try to claim as much parking space as they could manage (without blocking common access), and, and have workshops and shops on a different floor.

How much each steward charges for parking is a result of supplies and demands.

Freedom of Access can only support that a person can reach a destination, not that their vehicle would have a place to park.

Whether people should have a place to park is a decision of the individual stewards.

If the stewards, as a result of how they collectively built the town, don't provide enough parking, it just means that they decide that is the environment they want to create.

The visitors can decide whether to visit and an equilbrium would be reached where each steward doesn't want to and doesn't need to provide more parking, and some visitors might not be able to visit because the stewards don't want to accommodate additional visitors.

This does not violate access because freedom of travel doesn't mean that anyone may enter anyone's stewarded space. The destination is valid only if it welcomes the visitor.

This situation is not different from any inn being full and decides not to house additional visitors.

Gaia Accounting is organic. It doesn't dictate what style the environment would become. It evolves into an equilibrium.

🛞 1.4.1.1. Huge Parking Lot

A huge parking lot may exist with Gaia Accounting when the steward of the lot has enough Gaia Shares to reserve the space as a parking lot.

This normally means that the stewards must be providing something valuable enough that it can "afford" to "waste space" on parking lot.

🐾 1.4.1.2. No Parking

Having no parking at all is also possible. It happens when the steward has enough visitors anyway, where visitor can get there by themselves or be carried by a vehicle that doesn't need to park on site.

Again, the Steward decides how much traffic they want to serve.

2. Protocol

This section is about what to do for this survey quest.

🍀 2.1. Collect

Collect and summarize ideas on parking policies.

❤️ 2.2. Mediate

Account for concerns, expecially those that correspond to a violation.

:Paladin: 2.3. Evaluate

Evaluate the idea and give a verdict on whether is has any violation.

🔮 2.4. Predict

When applicable, predict whether the intended effect would result from the design.

Just because a plan is clear of violations doesn't mean that it would yield the intended benefit.

📯 2.5. Share

Show the list of violation-free proposals and help people implement them.

🎩 2.6. Engage

Engage people to bring awareness and help.
